The following paintings were painted by Paul Klee. Please read the explanation about Klee’s painting style:

“Paul Klee, a Swiss-born painter, printmaker, and draughtsman of German nationality, was originally associated with the German Expressionist group Der Blaue Reiter, and subsequently taught at the Bauhaus, the widely influential German art school of the interwar period. Klee's diverse body of work cannot, however, be categorized ac-cording to any single artistic movement, or "school." His paintings, which are at times fantastic, childlike, or otherwise witty. Klee challenged traditional boundaries separating writing and visual art by exploring a new expressive, and largely abstract or poetic language of pictorial symbols and signs. Arrows, letters, musical notation, ancient hieroglyphs, or a few black lines standing in for a person or object frequently appear in his work, while rarely demanding a specific reading.”
